Emergency repairs

Fast help for leaks, clogs, and urgent issues.

We start with a quick diagnostic, explain what’s happening, then recommend the safest fix for your home.

Leak repair

Faucets, supply lines, traps, fittings—diagnosis and repair.

  • Stop-the-leak fixes and part replacement
  • Under-sink inspection for hidden drips
  • Test run + visual leak check

Clogs

Sink, tub, toilet—mechanical clearing and safe options as needed.

  • Best-effort clearing with the right tools
  • Advice to prevent repeat clogs
  • We avoid “miracle chemicals” when possible

Toilet internals

Fill valves, flappers, tank parts—adjustment and sealing.

  • Fix running toilets and weak flush
  • Adjustments for quiet, efficient operation
  • Check base stability and seal symptoms

Shutoffs & urgent water control

If water needs to be stopped quickly, we help locate and operate shutoffs safely.

  • Help identifying main and fixture shutoffs
  • Replace worn valves (when feasible)
  • Quick safety guidance to limit damage

Slow leaks & water stains

Small leaks can cause big damage. We track drips and fix the source.

  • Under-sink, toilet, and supply line checks
  • Visible leak testing after repair
  • Advice on what to monitor next

What to prepare

  • Share photos/video if you can
  • Clear under-sink items if the leak is there
  • Tell us if water is shut off already

What you can expect

  • Upfront explanation and options
  • Clean work area + basic cleanup
  • Leak test after repairs

Installations

Professional installs with leak testing.

Ideal for upgrades and replacements. We measure twice, install carefully, and test everything under pressure.

Faucets & traps

Install or replacement, test run, and tidy sealing.

  • Remove old hardware cleanly
  • Update supply lines if needed
  • Check for drips after hot/cold run

Toilets

Install, connect, test, and seal the base correctly.

  • Set, level, and stabilize the bowl
  • Connect and test the supply
  • Verify flush + check base seal symptoms

Water heaters

Water connections, safety components, and first start-up.

  • Replace valves/lines as appropriate
  • Confirm proper operation at start-up
  • Basic guidance on maintenance

Shutoff valves & supply lines

Replace worn valves and lines to reduce risk of sudden leaks.

  • Fixture shutoff replacement (as applicable)
  • New braided supply lines when needed
  • Leak check under pressure

Sinks & under-sink rebuilds

New sink installs and clean under-sink plumbing updates.

  • Trap alignment and proper sealing
  • Replace worn fittings as needed
  • Test run + drip inspection

Appliance hookups

Hookups done correctly to protect your equipment.

Washer/dishwasher

Connect to supply and drain, then check for leaks.

  • New hoses/lines if needed
  • Secure drain connection
  • Cycle test + leak check

Water filtration

Install, cartridge replacement, and maintenance tips.

  • Under-sink or inline systems (as applicable)
  • Help selecting replacement cartridges
  • Check for drips after pressurizing

Garbage disposals

Connect to sink, drain, and power (where applicable).

  • Mount, seal, and align properly
  • Verify drain flow and connections
  • Basic do/don’t usage tips

Fridge / ice maker lines

New water line run or replacement, then pressure test.

  • Replace old plastic lines when possible
  • Proper routing to prevent kinks
  • Final leak check at the connection

Softeners (where applicable)

Installation support and maintenance help for hard-water areas.

  • Hookups and basic setup checks
  • Advice on salt and routine care
  • Leak check after pressurizing

Service questions

Quick answers before you book.

Do you provide parts?

Yes. We can bring recommended parts or install customer-supplied parts when appropriate.

How do you price jobs?

We confirm pricing after a quick diagnostic and before work begins. Complexity, access, and parts affect the final total.

Do you offer a warranty?

Warranty options may be available depending on the service. Terms are confirmed before we start.

How we estimate

For a more accurate estimate, a photo/video or a short description is usually enough.

  • What’s broken / what needs installing
